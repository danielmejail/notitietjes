Let $X$ be a topological space.
\begin{enumerate}[(i)]
	\item\label{item:ejer:capi:20:i}
		If $Y$ is an irreducible (Exercise~\ref{ejer:capi:19}) subspace
		of $X$, then the closure $\clos Y$ of $Y$ in $X$ is
		irreducible.
	\item\label{item:ejer:capi:20:ii}
		Every irreducible subspace of $X$ is contained in a maximal
		irreducible subspace.
	\item\label{item:ejer:capi:20:iii}
		The maximal irreducible subspaces of $X$ are closed and cover
		$X$. They are called the \emph{irreducible components of $X$}.
		What are the irreducible components of a Hausdorff space?
	\item\label{item:ejer:capi:20:iv}
		If $A$ is a ring and $X=\espectro A$, then the irreducible
		components of $X$ are the closed sets $\ceros{\frak p}$, where
		$\frak p$ is a minimal prime ideal of $A$ (Exercise~%
		\ref{ejer:capi:08}).
\end{enumerate}
