Let $A$ be a ring and let $A[\![x]\!]$ be the ring of formal power series
$f=\sum_{n\geq 0}\,a_n\,x^n$ with coefficients in $A$. Show that:
\begin{enumerate}[(i)]
	\item\label{item:ejer:capi:05:i}
		$f$ is a unit in $A[\![x]\!]$, if and only if $a_0$ is a unit
		in $A$;
	\item\label{item:ejer:capi:05:ii}
		if $f$ is nilpotent, then $a_n$ is nilpotent for all $n\geq 0$.
		Is the converse true? (See Exercise~\ref{ejer:capvii:02})
	\item\label{item:ejer:capi:05:iii}
		$f$ belongs to the Jacobson radical of $A[\![x]\!]$, if and
		only if $a_0$ belongs to the Jacobson radical of $A$;
	\item\label{item:ejer:capi:05:iv}
		the contraction of a maximal ideal $\frak m$ of $A[\![x]\!]$ is
		a maximal ideal of $A$, and $\frak m$ is generated by
		$\contracted{\frak m}$ and $x$;
	\item\label{item:ejer:capi:05:v}
		every prime ideal of $A$ is the contraction of a prime ideal of
		$A[\![x]\!]$.
\end{enumerate}
