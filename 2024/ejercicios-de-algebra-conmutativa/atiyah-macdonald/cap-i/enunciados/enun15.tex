Let $A$ be a ring and let $X$ be the set of all prime ideals of $A$. For each
subset $E$ of $A$, let $\ceros E$ denote the set of all prime ideals of $A$
which contain $E$. Prove that
\begin{enumerate}[(i)]
	\item\label{item:ejer:capi:15:i}
		if $\frak a$ is the ideal generated by $E$, then
		$\ceros E=\ceros{\frak a}=\ceros{\rad\,\frak a}$;
	\item\label{item:ejer:capi:15:ii}
		$\ceros 0=X$, $\ceros 1=\varnothing$;
	\item\label{item:ejer:capi:15:iii}
		if $\{E_i\}_i$ is any family of subsets of $A$, then
		\begin{align*}
			\ceros{\bigcup_i\,E_i} & \,=\,\bigcap_i\,\ceros{E_i}
			\text{ ;}
		\end{align*}
		%
	\item\label{item:ejer:capi:15:iv}
		\begin{math}
			\ceros{\frak a\cap\frak b}=\ceros{\frak a\,\frak b}=
				\ceros{\frak a}\cup\ceros{\frak b}
		\end{math}.
\end{enumerate}
%
These results show that the sets $\ceros E$ satisfy the axioms for closed sets
in a topological space. The resulting topology is called the \emph{Zariski %
topology}. The topological space $X$ is called \emph{the prime spectrum of %
$A$}, and is written $\espectro(A)$.
